Ethnic Encampments
Red Mill Gallery, Vermont Studio Center, Spring, 1999

The figures for this "encampment" are made from hardware cloth, and have a small stone suspended from string inside their chests. When the figures are moved back and forth, the "hearts" beat in their chests. The pieces for the Enthnic Encampments installation were created during "Vermont Week" at the Vermont Studio Center in 1999. The small figures (6-18" high) were constructed of wood, metal, clay, and fiber. Each "encampment" contained from from 3-8 figures with a similar morphology (their "ethnicity"), and they were displayed in separate groupings around the wall, or in long lines, as references to the refugees in the Balkans.
